# Lumenora Blog + Quiz — Editorial Commerce Mockups

## Objective

Create two high-fidelity, full-page desktop mockup images for Lumenora: one Blog landing page and one Skin Quiz landing page. Both must feel like natural extensions of the current Home editorial design and Shop All luxury marketplace, while using imagery and useful UI components to avoid large empty areas.

## Deliverables

- `Mockups/blog-editorial-commerce-full.png`
- `Mockups/quiz-editorial-commerce-full.png`
- Each image is a straight-on, tall desktop webpage at a 9:16 portrait ratio, targeting 2160 × 3840 px.
- No browser chrome, device frame, perspective presentation, watermark, or surrounding mockup scene.
- English interface copy, consistent with the existing Lumenora screens.

## Shared Visual System

- Direction: luxury botanical skincare presented as an editorial beauty publication with commerce woven into the reading and discovery flow.
- Palette: paper ivory `#F4F0E8`, parchment `#E8E0D2`, charcoal `#181713`, botanical olive `#596047`, warm amber glass, oxidized brass, and restrained oxblood `#6B1F2B` accents.
- Typography: high-contrast serif for display headings; compact uppercase sans-serif for navigation, labels, metadata, and buttons.
- UI details: squared imagery, thin hairline dividers, small tracked labels, restrained rectangular buttons, minimal corner radius, no glossy gradients, no oversized pill components.
- Photography: warm natural sunlight, limestone, amber or frosted glass, botanical shadows, cream textures, realistic skin, and subtle film grain.
- Page density: imagery occupies roughly 55–60% of each page; sections are compact and clearly separated without oversized blank bands.
- Header: centered `LUMENORA` wordmark; `HOME`, `SHOP`, `QUIZ`, `BLOG`; Search, Wishlist, and Cart utility actions. The current page is visibly active.
- Footer: charcoal background, ivory wordmark, short newsletter field, and only existing destinations: `SHOP`, `QUIZ`, `BLOG`, `WISHLIST`, `CART`.

## Blog Mockup

### Purpose

Turn the Blog landing page into a premium editorial hub that balances education, visual storytelling, and light product discovery. The page must be easy to scan and noticeably denser than the current Blog mockup.

### Page Structure

1. **Header and category rail**
   - Active `BLOG` navigation state.
   - Compact category rail: `LATEST`, `INGREDIENTS`, `ROUTINES`, `SKIN HEALTH`, `SUNCARE`, `BODY`.
   - Thin dividers and no large empty header band.

2. **Lead editorial story**
   - Asymmetric split: approximately 60% large skincare editorial image and 40% oxblood or charcoal text panel.
   - Eyebrow: `THE LUMENORA JOURNAL`.
   - Headline: `The Quiet Science of a Stronger Skin Barrier`.
   - Short deck, author/date metadata, and `READ THE STORY` button.
   - Image direction: luminous close-up skin, serum texture, soft botanical shadow, premium magazine crop.

3. **Latest stories mosaic**
   - One wide feature, two portrait stories, and one compact text-led story arranged on an asymmetric twelve-column grid.
   - Use these existing article titles where possible:
     - `Why Snail Mucin is the Skincare Ingredient You Can't Ignore`
     - `The Ultimate Guide to Double Cleansing`
     - `How to Repair a Damaged Skin Barrier`
     - `Building a Deep Hydration Routine for Dry Skin`
   - Every story includes a category label and concise metadata. Avoid equal-height card rows.

4. **Ingredient index**
   - Olive editorial band with close macro imagery of mugwort, snail mucin texture, volcanic clay, and birch water.
   - Four compact index entries separated by hairlines: `MUGWORT`, `SNAIL MUCIN`, `VOLCANIC CLAY`, `BIRCH`.
   - Include one short sentence and arrow action per entry.

5. **Routine desk**
   - Two-column composition: editorial bathroom or vanity scene beside a numbered but compact `MORNING / EVENING` routine comparison.
   - Show four concise steps with small product thumbnails: Cleanse, Treat, Moisturize, Protect.
   - Maintain magazine styling rather than dashboard styling.

6. **Most read + editor's selection**
   - Left: ranked `MOST READ` list with five article headlines and thin rules.
   - Right: original amber-glass product still life with `THE EDITOR'S SHELF`, three product captions, visible prices, and a restrained `SHOP THE EDIT` action.
   - Commerce remains secondary to editorial content.

7. **Newsletter closing and footer**
   - Full-width oxblood or deep olive strip with `NOTES FROM THE BEAUTY DESK`, compact email field, and `SUBSCRIBE` button.
   - Transition directly into the shared charcoal footer.

## Quiz Mockup

### Purpose

Make the Skin Quiz page feel useful before the user starts: visually explain the personalized result, preview the interaction, and demonstrate the routine outcome. Replace the current nearly empty hero with a component-rich editorial journey.

### Page Structure

1. **Header and immersive hero**
   - Active `QUIZ` navigation state.
   - Approximately 55/45 split: text and quiz preview on the left, editorial skincare image on the right.
   - Eyebrow: `PERSONALIZED FOR YOU`.
   - Headline: `Discover Your Signature Routine`.
   - Supporting copy explains a five-question analysis and four-step result.
   - Primary CTA: `BEGIN ANALYSIS`; secondary text action: `SEE HOW IT WORKS`.
   - Image direction: diverse adult woman in warm window light, natural skin texture, amber serum and cream vessel nearby, botanical shadow.

2. **Compact quiz preview panel**
   - Visible inside or directly below the hero so the page immediately communicates interaction.
   - Progress label `QUESTION 01 OF 05` with a slim progress bar.
   - Prompt: `How does your skin usually feel by midday?`
   - Four illustrated selection cards: `BALANCED`, `DRY OR TIGHT`, `OILY`, `COMBINATION`.
   - One selected state in botanical olive; compact `CONTINUE` button.

3. **How it works**
   - Three editorial steps separated by rules rather than floating cards:
     - `01 — TELL US ABOUT YOUR SKIN`
     - `02 — WE MAP YOUR PRIORITIES`
     - `03 — RECEIVE YOUR RITUAL`
   - Pair the steps with macro images of skin, serum droplets, and botanical formulation.

4. **Concern selector mosaic**
   - Heading: `What would you like to focus on?`
   - Six image-backed selection tiles: `DEHYDRATION`, `SENSITIVITY`, `DULLNESS`, `TEXTURE`, `BREAKOUTS`, `FINE LINES`.
   - Vary tile ratios to avoid a repetitive grid, but preserve obvious selectable states.

5. **Result preview**
   - Strong parchment section titled `YOUR FOUR-STEP RITUAL`.
   - Four products displayed in a staggered editorial composition with concise roles: `CLEANSE`, `TREAT`, `MOISTURIZE`, `PROTECT`.
   - Include product name, one-line rationale, and price under each image.
   - Add a small skin-profile summary: `COMBINATION · DEHYDRATED · SENSITIVE`.

6. **Trust and testimonial band**
   - One short testimonial beside a natural portrait crop.
   - Three concise reassurance points: `5 QUESTIONS`, `UNDER 2 MINUTES`, `PERSONALIZED ROUTINE`.
   - Avoid unsupported medical or diagnostic claims; describe the result as guided product discovery.

7. **Final CTA and footer**
   - Deep olive or charcoal section with product still life and headline `Your ritual begins with understanding.`
   - Button: `START THE QUIZ`.
   - Transition directly into the shared footer without a large empty spacer.

## Image Generation Strategy

- Generate each page as one integrated UI mockup using the built-in image generation tool.
- Use the current Home and Shop All mockups as visual references for brand language, header, typography, palette, photography, and footer treatment; do not copy their exact layouts.
- Important headings and primary CTA labels must be readable. Keep dense microcopy minimal because generated small text is less reliable.
- Generate original Lumenora-style photography inside the composition; do not reproduce real skincare brand packaging or logos.
- Save outputs non-destructively using the two approved filenames.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Exactly two distinct full-page desktop images are delivered: Blog and Quiz.
- Both images visibly belong to the same design system as the current Home and Shop All pages.
- Each page uses imagery and useful UI components throughout, with no conspicuous empty middle sections.
- Blog reads primarily as an editorial publication, with commerce as a supporting layer.
- Quiz communicates a clear start state, representative question interaction, process explanation, and personalized four-step outcome.
- All major section headings, navigation labels, and primary CTA labels are readable.
- Layouts are straight-on, implementation-oriented webpage designs rather than atmospheric concept art.
- No browser chrome, device frame, watermark, copied brand identity, or unsupported medical claim appears.
