# Shop All Editorial Marketplace Mockup Design

**Date:** 2026-07-17  
**Status:** Approved design, pending written-spec review  
**Deliverable:** One desktop full-page 4K mockup image sent directly in chat

## Goal

Create a polished Shop All mockup that makes product discovery fast and intuitive while matching the established Home page. The page should feel like a refined multi-brand beauty marketplace, not a decorative campaign page. Editorial styling supports the shopping experience but never interrupts comparison or navigation.

## Canvas and Scope

- Desktop full-page canvas at 2160 x 3840 pixels.
- Show the complete page from the existing header through the footer.
- Use English interface copy to match the current Home page.
- Use fictional brands and products only.
- Product imagery should look like premium 4K studio photography embedded in the mockup.
- This deliverable is a static visual concept, not an implemented interface.

## Visual Direction

The approved direction is **Editorial Marketplace**. It combines the warm editorial character of Home with a restrained commerce layout.

### Palette

- Ivory `#F5F1E8` for the main page background.
- Parchment `#E8E0D1` for subtle section separation and product-photo backdrops.
- Charcoal `#25231F` for primary text and controls.
- Deep olive for selected categories and quiet brand accents.
- Oxblood for important hover or call-to-action emphasis.
- Brass for small labels, rules, and editorial details only.

### Typography

- Playfair Display or a visually equivalent editorial serif for page headings.
- Inter or a visually equivalent neutral sans-serif for filters, prices, metadata, and actions.
- Strong hierarchy, generous spacing, and high legibility take priority over decorative type treatments.

### Materials and Photography

Fictional skincare, body care, sun care, and fragrance products should include amber-glass bottles, minimal cream tubes, serum droppers, jars, and perfume vessels. Images use soft directional sunlight, travertine, linen, olive leaves, and occasional oxblood fabric. Packaging remains logo-free or uses clearly fictional minimal labels. Product scenes vary slightly while retaining consistent lighting, palette, and scale.

## Page Structure

### 1. Existing Header

Preserve the current Lumenora header language so the transition from Home feels continuous. Navigation, search, account, wishlist, and bag remain recognizable and visually quiet.

### 2. Compact Shop Introduction

Use a compact 640-pixel-tall hero so products remain close to the fold. The left side contains an editorial eyebrow, a large serif “Shop All” title, a short curation statement, and a concise product/brand count. The right side contains one premium still-life image using the approved materials and colors.

### 3. Sticky Discovery Toolbar

The toolbar stays visually distinct with a fine border and ivory background. It contains:

- Category tabs: All, Skin, Body, Sun, Fragrance.
- A clearly labeled “All filters” control.
- Product count.
- “Sort: Featured” control.

The All category is active in the mockup. Active state uses olive and a clear underline or border. Controls have a minimum visual target size of 44 pixels.

### 4. Filter Behavior

The mockup shows the default state with the filter panel closed, preserving a spacious four-column grid. The intended filter panel opens from the left and contains Category, Brand, Concern, Product Type, and Price, plus “Clear all” and “Show results” actions. When filters are active, removable chips appear above the grid.

### 5. Product Grid

Use a consistent four-column grid with generous gutters and calm vertical rhythm. Do not insert promotional banners between product rows because they interrupt comparison. Show 12 fictional products across three rows.

Each product card follows this information order:

1. Large product image.
2. Restrained New or Bestseller badge where relevant.
3. Fictional brand name.
4. Product name.
5. One short benefit or product-type line.
6. Price.
7. Wishlist and Quick add actions.

Cards use fine borders or open whitespace rather than drop shadows. One card shows a hover state with a secondary image and visible Quick add action; all essential product information remains visible without hover.

### 6. End-of-List Editorial Block

Place a single “The Lumenora Edit” section after the product list, never between product rows. Use a charcoal or deep olive background, a concise editorial message, and one restrained call to action. This provides brand atmosphere after the primary shopping task is complete.

### 7. Newsletter and Footer

Keep the newsletter and footer consistent with Home in typography, color, spacing, and information hierarchy.

## Usability Principles

- Keep products visible near the fold by limiting hero height.
- Make category, filter, sort, and result-count controls immediately discoverable.
- Preserve a stable four-column comparison grid in the default desktop state.
- Avoid visual interruptions inside the product list.
- Keep names, prices, and product types legible at full-page viewing scale.
- Use badges sparingly and do not cover meaningful product details.
- Maintain strong contrast and minimum 44-pixel action targets.
- Use color to communicate hierarchy and state, not as decoration alone.

## Static Mockup State

- Route concept: Shop All.
- Active category: All.
- Sort: Featured.
- Filter drawer: Closed.
- Product grid: Four columns.
- One product card: Hover state shown for interaction illustration.
- Product copy: English and fictional.
- Output: One 2160 x 3840 full-page image displayed directly in the Codex chat.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The mockup is immediately recognizable as part of the current Lumenora Home experience.
- Product discovery and comparison are visually more important than editorial decoration.
- Header, discovery toolbar, product cards, end-of-list editorial block, and footer form a coherent full page.
- The 4K image is sharp enough to evaluate spacing, type hierarchy, card anatomy, color, and product photography.
- All depicted product brands and packaging are fictional and contain no third-party logos.
- The image contains no watermarks, distorted interface chrome, or unrelated visual elements.
